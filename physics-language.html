IB DP Physics — Critical Language Analysis

The Language
of Physics

When a physicist says heat "flows" or nature "prefers" low energy — what kind of claim is that?

Physics is full of statements that work — that generate correct predictions, guide experiments, and structure understanding. But working and being literally true are not the same thing. This resource examines typical and less-obvious examples from across the IB DP physics syllabus where metaphorical, teleological, or conceptually loaded language shapes how students reason.

Each statement is read through three lenses. The mechanistic explanation states what the model actually says: the equation, the conditions, the formal mechanism. Cartwright's critique asks how model-dependent the claim is — does it presuppose ideal conditions, import assumptions silently, or assert a domain-specific result as universal? Wittgenstein's perspective asks what the language itself is doing: which metaphors are operating, what images they invoke, and where those images mislead.

The aim is not to undermine physics, but to make its language visible — and thereby more precise.
