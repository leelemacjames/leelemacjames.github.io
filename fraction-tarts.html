§ 03 — The ReasoningHow to compare without decimals

The Egyptian decomposition gives you a principled way to compare fractions. The first unit fraction in each expansion is the most important — it tells you which "shelf" the fraction lives on.

Step 1 — Simplify first

Always simplify before decomposing. 812 = 23. This immediately places it: it is exactly two thirds.

Step 2 — Greedy first term

The greedy first term tells you the coarsest unit fraction that fits. Fractions with the same first term are close together; fractions with different first terms are well-separated.

Step 3 — Unit fraction number line

All three fractions shown against a 0 → 1 line. The first unit fraction in each expansion is marked. Fractions landing near the same unit fraction marker are close; those whose first term differs by one step are already well-separated.
