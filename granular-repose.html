Liquid bridges form at grain contacts via capillary forces, adding attraction on top of dry friction. More cohesion means grains resist sliding further, lifting both repose angles. Toasted dry couscous lacks these bridges entirely — hence it flows more freely.
Static friction exceeds kinetic friction at every grain contact. The pile needs a steeper angle to start moving than to keep moving. A slope inside the hysteresis window is metastable — it will not spontaneously avalanche, but if disturbed it will not stop either.

The Physics of Granular Repose

Angle of Repose

The steepest slope a granular pile maintains without avalanching, set by the ratio of inter-grain friction to the gravitational component along the slope.

tan θ = μs + C/(ρgr)

Static vs Kinetic

Static friction resists the onset of sliding; kinetic friction governs grains already in motion. The difference produces a hysteresis window between triggering and stopping an avalanche.

θstatic > θkinetic

Cohesion & Capillary Bridges

Thin liquid films at grain contacts add capillary attraction. The effective friction rises, lifting both repose angles. Dry toasted grains lack these bridges and flow more freely.

Fcap ~ 2πγr cos θc
