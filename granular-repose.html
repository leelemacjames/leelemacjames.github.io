Insight

Static friction > kinetic friction at grain contacts. So grains need a steeper angle to start moving than to keep moving. The gap between the two angles is the hysteresis window — a slope inside it is metastable.
Liquid bridges form at grain contacts, creating capillary forces that add to friction. More cohesion means grains resist sliding more, so the static repose angle increases. Dry toasted couscous removes these bridges.

The Physics of Granular Repose

Angle of Repose

The steepest slope a granular pile maintains without avalanching. Set by the ratio of inter-grain friction to the gravitational component along the slope.

tan θ = μs + C/(ρgr)

Static vs Kinetic

Static friction resists the onset of sliding; kinetic friction (lower) governs grains already moving. The difference produces a hysteresis window between triggering and stopping an avalanche.

θstatic > θkinetic

Cohesion & Capillary Bridges

Thin liquid films at grain contacts add an attractive force. The effective friction rises, raising both repose angles. Dry (toasted) grains lack these bridges and flow more freely.

Fcap ~ 2πγr · cos θc
