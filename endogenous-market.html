Endogenous Market Simulation

Agent-based pricing with fundamental vs. technical traders on epistemic networks

The Model

This simulation constructs a market from first principles. There is no exogenous price feed that agents passively observe — instead, price is set by aggregate demand. Each agent holds a belief about an asset's fundamental value, computes a desired portfolio weight via the Markowitz formula, and submits demand. The market-clearing mechanism aggregates all demand and reprices the asset accordingly.

Two classes of agent populate the network. Fundamental agents receive noisy signals about the asset's true value (anchored to real FTSE100 price data) and update their beliefs via Bayes' or Jeffrey's rule, sharing evidence with their network neighbours. Technical agents receive no exogenous signal — they update beliefs solely from the observed market price and its recent history (a moving average crossover). They are the degenerate case: no independent evidence, only endogenous feedback.

The real FTSE 100 daily closes (Aug 2008 - Jul 2023, covering the financial crisis, recovery, Brexit, COVID, and post-COVID) serve as the fundamental value trajectory — the ground truth that fundamental agents can (noisily) observe but technical agents cannot. The market price that results from the agents' collective demand may deviate from this fundamental value. The gap between the two is the mispricing — the measurable cost of network structure, trust dynamics, and the proportion of technical traders.

The Simulation

Each step, every agent computes a desired portfolio weight from their belief about value. Excess aggregate demand drives price: price(t+1) = price(t) · (1 + η · excess_demand). Fundamental value evolves exogenously (real FTSE data). The question is how quickly the market price tracks it.

% Technical: fraction using price-only signals. At 0%, all fundamental — price tracks value. At 100%, pure self-reference. η: how strongly demand moves price. σ_s: noise on fundamental signal. λ: risk aversion — how aggressively beliefs become weights. Update rule: Bayes (full trust) vs Jeffrey (discount by disagreement).

Reading the price chart

Green line: the real FTSE 100 daily close — the asset's fundamental value. Gold line: the price generated endogenously by agent demand. The shaded red area between them is mispricing. In a well-functioning market (fundamental agents, good topology, low noise), the gold line hugs the green. As technical trader proportion rises or trust degrades, the gold line diverges — sometimes permanently.

Reading the belief chart

Blue line: mean belief of fundamental agents — should track the green (truth). Red line: mean belief of technical agents — tracks price, not value. The shaded band is ±1 standard deviation across all agents. When the band is wide, agents disagree — the network has not reached consensus. When blue and red diverge, the two agent classes are in conflict, and the market price is a weighted average of irreconcilable views — the phantom portfolio from the paper.
