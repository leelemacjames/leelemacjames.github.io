Insight

Gravity force scales as r³ (volume), surface tension pinning as r (contact line). So the ratio gravity/pinning grows as r². Above a critical radius the droplet unpins and slides, sweeping up smaller neighbours.

Roughly where mg sinθ > 2γ(cosθR − cosθA)w, where γ is surface tension, θA, θR are advancing/receding contact angles, and w is droplet width. In this model, rcrit = surf × 15px.

The Competing Forces

Gravity

Acts on the droplet's volume. A larger droplet feels proportionally more gravitational pull, driving it downward against the glass surface.

Fg = ρg · (4/3)πr³ sinθ

Surface Tension Pinning

Microscopic defects and contact angle hysteresis pin small droplets. The resisting force scales with the contact line length, not volume.

Fpin ~ 2γw(cosθR − cosθA)

Coalescence

When a sliding droplet overlaps a pinned one, they merge. Volume (hence r³) is conserved. The merged droplet continues, now larger and faster.

rnew = (r1³ + r2³)1/3
