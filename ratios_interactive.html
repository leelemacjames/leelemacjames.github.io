The Language of Ratios

From simplifying and sharing to the geometry of congruence and similarity — ratios as a unified idea.

Simplifying Ratios

Dividing both parts of a ratio by their Highest Common Factor (HCF) gives the simplest form. Think of it as cancelling a fraction, but for two quantities.

Method
  • 1
    Write the ratio a : b
  • 2
    Find the HCF of a and b — the largest number that divides both exactly
  • 3
    Divide both parts by the HCF
  • 4
    Check neither part shares any common factor ≥ 2
Worked Example
Simplify 8 : 2
Factors of 8: 1, 2, 4, 8
Factors of 2: 1, 2
HCF = 2
8 ÷ 2 = 4  |  2 ÷ 2 = 1
8 : 2  →  4 : 1
a : b → (a ÷ HCF) : (b ÷ HCF)

Unitary Method

Convert any ratio to the form 1 : n by dividing both sides by the first part. This makes comparison between different ratios instant.

Method — 1 : n form
  • 1
    Start with ratio a : b
  • 2
    Divide both sides by a
  • 3
    Result: 1 : (b/a) — now n can be a decimal or fraction
a : b → 1 : (b ÷ a)

Sharing in a Given Ratio

Divide a total amount into parts according to a ratio. The total is split into "shares" and each person/part gets the right number of shares.

Method
  • 1
    Add the ratio parts → total number of shares
  • 2
    Divide the total by the number of shares → value of 1 share
  • 3
    Multiply each ratio part by the value of 1 share
  • 4
    Check: the parts should sum back to the original total
Example — Share £30 in ratio 2 : 3
Total shares: 2 + 3 = 5
Value of 1 share: £30 ÷ 5 = £6
Part A: 2 × £6 = £12
Part B: 3 × £6 = £18
Check: £12 + £18 = £30 ✓

Congruence & Ratios

Congruent shapes are identical in size and shape. Their side ratios are all 1 : 1 — the ultimate simplest ratio.

Congruent

  • SSS — all 3 sides equal
  • SAS — 2 sides + included angle
  • AAS — 2 angles + a side
  • RHS — right angle, hypotenuse, side
Ratio of all corresponding sides = 1 : 1

Similar

  • All angles equal
  • Sides in constant ratio k : 1
  • Can be reflection / rotation
  • Scale factor k = enlarged ÷ original
Ratio of corresponding sides = k : 1

Similarity & Scale Factors

Similar shapes have equal angles but sides in a constant ratio — the scale factor k. Ratios are the mathematical backbone of all enlargements.

Scale Factor Formula
Scale factor k = (length on enlargement) ÷ (corresponding length on original)

PQ/AB = QR/BC = PR/AC = k

If k > 1 → enlargement  |  k = 1 → congruent  |  k < 1 → reduction

Which shapes are always similar?
✓ Always Any two squares, any two equilateral triangles, any two circles, any two cubes
→ These have only one "shape" — all sides/angles are fixed by one parameter
✗ Not always Rectangles, isosceles triangles, cylinders
→ These have two independent parameters (e.g. width & height for a rectangle), so side ratios can differ

🔗 The big picture: All ratio methods — simplifying (finding HCF), unitary form (dividing by first part), sharing (dividing by total parts) — are the same underlying operation: scaling both sides by a common factor. In geometry, that scaling factor becomes the scale factor k of an enlargement. Ratios are not a separate topic — they're the language of proportional thinking throughout mathematics.
