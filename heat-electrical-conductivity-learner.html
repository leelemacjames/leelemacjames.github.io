Similarities & Differences
Aspect Heat Conduction Electrical Conduction S / D
Equation form Rate ∝ A·ΔX / L Rate ∝ A·ΔX / L Similar
Carrier in metals Free electrons AND lattice vibrations (phonons) Free electrons only Differ
What flows Energy (joules) Charge (coulombs) Differ
Can flow in vacuum? No (conduction requires matter) No (electrons need a medium) Similar
Good conductors Metals (high k); insulators include wood, air Metals (high σ); insulators include rubber, glass Similar
Effect of temperature ↑ k roughly constant for metals (slight ↓) Resistance ↑ → conductivity ↓ (lattice scatters electrons more) Differ
Can transfer without medium? No — radiation does that instead No — radiation does not carry charge the same way Similar
Wiedemann-Franz link In metals, k/σ ≈ L₀T (Lorenz number). The same free electrons responsible for electrical conduction also dominate thermal conduction — they are coupled. Similar
Non-metals Can conduct heat (via phonons) — e.g. diamond is excellent Non-metals generally insulators (no free charges) Differ
