Compression & Expansion

On the Explanatory Modes of Physics and Biology

Why can physicists write equations, and biologists mostly cannot? The standard answer invokes complexity: biological systems have too many variables. This is true but insufficient. The deeper reason is closure.

A physical system like a charged particle in a magnetic field, or two masses under gravity, admits a small number of variables constrained by conservation laws and symmetries. Under these conditions, description compresses to algebra. The mechanism is implicit in the mathematical structure: a minus sign encodes opposition, a mass cancellation encodes geometric universality, a cross product encodes a three-way directional constraint. Nothing is lost in the compression. The equation is the explanation.

F = qv × B  —  three vectors, one relation, complete.
a = GM/r²  —  mass cancels, universality falls out.
ε = −dΦ/dt  —  a sign enforces conservation.

Biology operates in a different regime. Its systems are open, high-dimensional, path-dependent, and historically contingent. A gene regulation network, a metabolic pathway, an ecosystem: none can be compressed into a single invariant relation without losing precisely the information that matters. Life on Earth is the product of Darwinian selection acting on accident, not of symmetry principles acting on invariants. The organisms, pathways, and structures that biologists study were not derived from first principles; they were arrived at through a contingent, branching history of variation and differential survival. Their forms are not necessary but selected. Their explanations therefore mirror this origin: expanded, conditional, narrative, context-dependent.

Physics explains by compressing to what must be true. Biology explains by reconstructing how something came to be true, given that it might easily have been otherwise. The mathematisability of physics is more than a stylistic preference being rather a consequence of the closure that allows invariant relations to exist. The narrative character of biology is a faithful reflection of the contingency of its subject matter that renders its mathematisation difficult.

Physics

Minimal variables. Exact relations. Invariant under transformation. Mechanism compressed into algebra. Description is explanation.

Biology

Many variables. Interaction networks. Context-dependent. Mechanism expanded into narrative. Description and explanation remain distinct layers.
