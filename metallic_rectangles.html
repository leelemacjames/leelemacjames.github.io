Rectangle Self-Similarity Under Cutting

Golden · Silver · remove squares and the same shape remains
The core idea

A golden rectangle (ratio φ ≈ 1.618) reproduces itself when one square is removed from it. A silver rectangle (ratio δ = 1+√2 ≈ 2.414) reproduces itself when two adjacent squares are removed. Both are metallic numbers — solutions to x = n + 1/x for integers n. The self-similar cut can be repeated indefinitely.

The Collinearity Property

The metallic ratio is the unique x for which the three corner points are collinear
What the diagram shows

Stack the L-shaped pair of rectangles corner-to-corner as shown. For a generic x the diagonal of the combined figure does not pass through the interior corner. The golden ratio φ is the unique positive value of x for which all three labelled vertices are exactly collinear — the same line threads through every junction. The silver ratio δ = 1+√2 plays the same role in the analogous 2-square construction.

When does the large diagonal equal the sum of the two sub-diagonals?
