Bunge's Three Trajectories

Mario Bunge proposed that genuine progress in physics is not merely accumulative. It moves along three distinct axes simultaneously. Each unification in the history of physics can be assessed against all three.

Trajectory 1

Remains Physical

Physics should concern itself with physical objects, not observers. The theory speaks about what is out there, not about what we happen to measure. Observer-dependence is a symptom, not a feature.

Trajectory 2

Goes Deeper

From phenomenological to mechanistic. We don't just describe patterns; we explain them. Kepler's laws are phenomenological. Newton's gravitational mechanism is deeper. Einstein's geometric mechanism deeper still.

Trajectory 3

Becomes More Cogent

Formulated in more consistent, better organised ways. The mathematical scaffolding tightens. Invariants replace coordinate-dependent statements. Symmetry principles replace ad hoc rules.

Three Great Unifications

Each unification collapsed a false dichotomy. Click any entry to see how it scores on Bunge's three trajectories.

c. 1680

Unification of Forces of Heaven and Earth

▸ click to expand

Newton showed that the force pulling an apple downward and the force curving the Moon's path are the same. Terrestrial and celestial mechanics became one discipline.

Physical ✓
Mass as a quality of matter is attractive. Inertial and gravitational mass assumed equivalent. Forces act at a distance instantaneously (problematic, but the object is physical, not the observer).
Mechanistic ✓
a = F/m gives a mechanism: force causes acceleration. But "force at a distance" remains phenomenological. Why does gravity reach across space? Newton himself called it an "absurdity."
Cogent ✓
Three laws, one gravitational law, the whole solar system falls out. Static space provides the stage. But absolute space and time are extra structure that will later prove unnecessary.
c. 1850

Unification of Electricity & Magnetism through Fields

▸ click to expand

Maxwell unified Faraday's field concept into four equations. Light turned out to be an electromagnetic wave. The field became a physical entity in its own right, not merely a mathematical convenience.

Physical ✓
Fields are all-pervading. Inertial motion given special status. But: fields were thought to propagate through a material aether, which doesn't exist. The physical object (the field) was right; the assumed medium was wrong.
Mechanistic ✓
Force at a distance replaced by differential potentials. Light is self-inducing, intertwining E and B fields. The mechanism is local propagation, not instantaneous action. Optics unified with field theory.
Cogent ✓
Four equations. c emerges as a constant. Light follows null geodesics. Light demarcates spatial causal connectivity. But the aether frame broke the symmetry Maxwell's own equations demanded.
c. 1910

Unification of Optics and Mechanics

▸ click to expand

Einstein's Special Relativity showed that the laws of optics and mechanics are consistent when you abandon absolute simultaneity. Then General Relativity absorbed gravity into the geometry of spacetime itself.

Physical ✓
Spacetime fibration: the picture is lost as time gains equal footing with space. Radiation needs no medium. Space is locally flat and Lorentzian. The equivalence principle: "Do not presume! Make it a Principle."
Mechanistic ✓
Invariant intervals across frames fix causal structure. Mass-energy bends spacetime. Conservation of energy and mass merged: a verb (energy) equated to a noun (mass). E = mc².
Cogent ✓
Lorentz transforms. No universal time. Light speed as invariant and maximum speed limit. Galileo's relativity recovered as an approximation. But: inertia and absolute acceleration left not fully explained according to Mach's Principle.

Bottled Theories

The Big, the Fast, and the Complex. Modern physics has successfully bottled several regimes. But no single bottle contains them all. Black holes sit at the intersection, demanding a theory that unifies quantum mechanics with general relativity.

⚛

Quantum Mechanics

The Small · Deterministic ↔ Many · Likely

Deterministic evolution of probability amplitudes. Unitary. Superposition. Measurement collapses to outcomes weighted by |ψ|².

⚡

Particle Physics

The Fast · Small · Unitary

QFT on flat spacetime. The Standard Model. Group theory as organising principle: SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1). Orthonormal states, creation and annihilation operators.

🌀

General Relativity

The Big · Massive · Conformal

Spacetime curvature as gravity. Information sinks at horizons. Classical. Deterministic. No built-in mechanism for quantum phenomena.

🔥

Thermodynamics

The Complex · Ensembles · Not Catastrophic

Emerges from statistical mechanics. Thermal radiators. Entropy. Connects to black holes via Bekenstein-Hawking: horizons have temperature and entropy.

📊

Statistical Mechanics

Many → Likely → Ensembles

Bridges micro and macro. Interacting systems. Partition functions. Phase transitions. The combinatorial heart of thermodynamics.

🕳

Black Holes

The Nexus · Information Sinks

Where GR, QM, and thermodynamics collide. Hawking radiation. Information paradox. Any "genie theory" must explain what happens here.

The Genie Candidates

To which thread do we dangle from? Four candidate programmes each propose a different fundamental structure from which spacetime, particles, and gravity might emerge. Click to explore each thread.

String

String / M-Theory

Witten, Green, Schwarz
▸ click to expand

Replaces point particles with one-dimensional strings. Vibration modes correspond to different particles. Requires extra spatial dimensions (10 or 11 in M-theory). Natural home for gravity: the graviton emerges as a closed-string mode. Landscape problem: ~10⁵⁰⁰ possible vacua.

Bunge score: Deep mechanistic explanation, but the extra dimensions and landscape raise cogency concerns. Physicality is strong (strings are objects), but untestable predictions weaken the programme.

Knot

Ashtekar's Variables

Ashtekar
▸ click to expand

Reformulates GR using connection variables instead of the metric. Makes GR look like a gauge theory (similar to Yang-Mills). Opens the door to canonical quantisation of gravity. The foundation on which Loop Quantum Gravity was built.

Bunge score: Excellent cogency (gauge-theoretic language). Mechanistic depth by making gravity's structure manifest. Physical: spacetime geometry rebuilt from connection and triad.

Loop

Loop Quantum Gravity

Smolin, Rovelli
▸ click to expand

Quantises spacetime directly. Spin networks replace smooth geometry. Area and volume become discrete, quantised. Background-independent: no fixed spacetime assumed. Predicts a minimum area (Planck scale). Struggles to recover smooth spacetime at large scales and to incorporate matter fields naturally.

Bunge score: Strongest on "remains physical" (quantises what we know exists: geometry). Mechanistic via discrete spectra. Cogency challenged by the semiclassical limit problem.

Twistor

Twistor Theory

Penrose
▸ click to expand

Replaces spacetime points with twistors: objects in a complex projective space. Light rays, not points, become fundamental. Conformally invariant. Naturally encodes massless field equations. Amplituhedron programme (Arkani-Hamed) connects to scattering amplitudes. Struggles with massive particles and curved spacetime.

Bunge score: Exceptional cogency (complex geometry, projective structure). Mechanistic depth through encoding physics in holomorphic functions. Physical: light rays as primary objects is radical but grounded.
