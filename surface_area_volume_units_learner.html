Surface Area, Volume & Units

A Year 10 Q&A learner for spheres, cylinders and cuboids, with a units bridge from microlitres (µL) to cubic centimetres (cm³). Built for preamble work before oil-film, density, pressure or power-law investigations.

Start: what are we measuring?

Length

A one-dimensional measurement. It measures a line distance.

cm, m, mm

Area

A two-dimensional measurement. It measures a surface.

cm², m², mm²

Volume

A three-dimensional measurement. It measures space occupied.

cm³, m³, mL, µL
The unit tells you the dimension: cm is length, cm² is area, cm³ is volume.
Q1. Which unit is used for volume?
Correct. Cubic centimetres measure volume.
Check the exponent. A cube unit has power 3, so it measures volume.

2. Cuboids: the easiest volume

Cuboid formulas

Volume is base area times height.

\(V = lwh\)

\(SA = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh\)

Surface area counts all six outside faces.

Q2. A cuboid is 4 cm by 3 cm by 2 cm. What is its volume?
Correct. Volume = 4 × 3 × 2 = 24 cm³.
Use three lengths: volume = length × width × height.

3. Cylinders: circular base times height

Cylinder formulas

A cylinder is like a stack of circular discs.

\(V = \pi r^2 h\)

\(\text{curved area} = 2\pi rh\)

\(\text{total SA} = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh\)

For many school problems, the volume formula is the main one.

Q3. A cylinder has radius 3 cm and height 5 cm. Which expression gives its volume?
Correct. Cylinder volume is πr²h.
Square the radius, then multiply by the height.

4. Spheres: surface grows as r², volume grows as r³

Sphere formulas

\(SA = 4\pi r^2\)

\(V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\)

If radius doubles, surface area becomes 4 times bigger and volume becomes 8 times bigger.

Q4. If a sphere's radius doubles, what happens to its volume?
Correct. Volume grows with r³, so doubling gives 2³ = 8.
Volume is three-dimensional, so use the cube: 2³.

5. Units: from µL to cm³

The essential conversion: 1 mL = 1 cm³. Since 1000 µL = 1 mL, it follows that 1000 µL = 1 cm³.

Microlitres to cm³

\(1\,\mu\text{L} = 0.001\,\text{cm}^3\)

Divide microlitres by 1000.

cm³ to microlitres

\(1\,\text{cm}^3 = 1000\,\mu\text{L}\)

Multiply cubic centimetres by 1000.

Common lab shortcut

\(1\,\mu\text{L} = 1\,\text{mm}^3\)

Useful because 1 cm³ = 1000 mm³.

Conversion machine

9 µL = 0.009 cm³ = 0.009 mL
Q5. 9 µL is equal to how many cm³?
Correct. Divide by 1000: 9 µL = 0.009 cm³.
Because 1000 µL = 1 cm³, microlitres are much smaller than cubic centimetres.

6. Oil film: volume spread over area

An oil drop spreads into a very thin circular film on water. You know the volume of oil dropped and the diameter of the film. Which formula lets you find the film thickness?

Choose the correct rearrangement to find thickness h:
Correct. The volume formula \(V = \pi r^2 h\) rearranges to \(h = \dfrac{V}{\pi r^2}\). The first option is the conceptual statement; the second has \(h\) on the wrong side.
Start from \(V = \pi r^2 h\) and divide both sides by \(\pi r^2\) to isolate \(h\).

7. Final Q&A check

Q6. Which formula gives the volume of a sphere?
Correct. Sphere volume is (4/3)πr³.
Look for the formula with r³, because volume is three-dimensional.
Q7. Which formula gives the surface area of a sphere?
Correct. Sphere surface area is 4πr².
Surface area is two-dimensional, so look for r².
Q8. 250 µL equals what volume?
Correct. 250 ÷ 1000 = 0.25 cm³.
Divide microlitres by 1000 to get cm³.
Q9. Why does volume grow faster than surface area for a sphere?
Correct. Cubing grows faster than squaring.
Compare the powers: r² for surface area, r³ for volume.
