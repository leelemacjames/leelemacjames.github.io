Euler, Platonic Solids & Atomic Shells

Euler Characteristic · Duality · Aufbau Principle · Zilong Kong's Mapping
The five Platonic solids obey V − E + F = 2. Their face counts decompose the electron shell capacities of every noble gas. Coincidence, or geometry all the way down?

Euler's Polyhedron Formula

For any convex polyhedron, the number of vertices, edges, and faces satisfies a topological invariant. This is the Euler characteristic of a sphere.

V − E + F = 2
Vertices minus Edges plus Faces always equals 2 for convex polyhedra

This isn't a coincidence of particular shapes. It's a topological fact: any convex polyhedron can be continuously deformed to a sphere, and the sphere has Euler characteristic χ = 2. The formula holds for the tetrahedron (4 − 6 + 4 = 2), the cube (8 − 12 + 6 = 2), and every Platonic solid.

✓ Euler characteristic = 2. This is a valid convex polyhedron.

Why 2? The Euler characteristic is a topological invariant. Cutting, stretching, or deforming a surface doesn't change it. A sphere has χ = 2. A torus has χ = 0. A double torus has χ = −2. The Platonic solids, being homeomorphic to the sphere, all give 2.

The Five Platonic Solids

Exactly five convex regular polyhedra exist. Each has identical regular polygonal faces, the same number of faces meeting at every vertex, and satisfies Euler's formula. Duality

Platonic solids come in dual pairs. To form the dual, replace every face with a vertex and every vertex with a face. Edges are preserved. The duality swaps F and V while keeping E constant.

Tetrahedron ↔ Tetrahedron

SELF-DUAL

F = 4, V = 4. Faces and vertices are equal, so the dual is itself. The only self-dual Platonic solid.

Cube ↔ Octahedron

F ↔ V : 6 ↔ 8  ·  E = 12

The cube has 6 faces and 8 vertices. Its dual, the octahedron, has 8 faces and 6 vertices. Both have 12 edges.

Dodecahedron ↔ Icosahedron

F ↔ V : 12 ↔ 20  ·  E = 30

The dodecahedron has 12 pentagonal faces and 20 vertices. Its dual, the icosahedron, has 20 triangular faces and 12 vertices.

Duality preserves Euler: If V − E + F = 2, then F − E + V = 2 too, since addition is commutative. Duality swaps V and F but Euler's formula is symmetric in that swap.

Duality also swaps

Edges-per-face ↔ Edges-per-vertex. The cube has 4 edges per face and 3 per vertex; the octahedron has 3 per face and 4 per vertex. This swap is the Schläfli symbol swap: {p, q} ↔ {q, p}.

The cumulative sequence 2, 8, 18, 32 gives the maximum shell capacities. The sequence of subshell sizes 2, 6, 10, 14 is the sequence of odd numbers (1, 3, 5, 7) doubled. This is where the Platonic face-count connection enters.

The group theory link: The rotation group SO(3) acts on the sphere via irreducible representations of dimension 2ℓ+1. Mark Ronan notes that "the group of rotations in 3-d has an operation in 5-d space," referring to the ℓ = 2 (d-orbital) representation. Each irreducible representation defines a subshell.

The Platonic Mapping

Zilong Kong's observation: the face counts of the Platonic solids (plus the cylinder) map directly onto the electron-shell-filling sequence of the noble gases. The electron configuration can be decomposed into Platonic face numbers.

How the faces map to electrons

s-orbital
Cylinder: 2 faces → 2 electrons. The s-orbital has spherical (1-dimensional rotational) symmetry. The cylinder captures this: top and bottom faces, like spin-up and spin-down.
2
p-orbital
Cube: 6 faces → 6 electrons. Three orthogonal p-orbitals (px, py, pz), each holding 2 electrons. The cube's 3 pairs of opposite faces mirror the 3 spatial axes.
6
d-orbital
Octahedron: 8 faces or Dodecahedron: 12 faces/edges. The d-orbitals' 5 orientations × 2 spins = 10 electrons. The connection to the octahedron's 8 faces captures the octahedral symmetry of crystal field theory.
10
f-orbital
Icosahedron: 20 faces. The 7 f-orbital orientations × 2 = 14 electrons. The icosahedron's 20 faces and the dodecahedron's 20 vertices provide the geometric skeleton.
14

The key insight: The standard shell sequence (2, 8, 18, 32…) bundles subshells together. Kong's decomposition un-bundles them, replacing each shell capacity with a sum of Platonic face/edge/vertex counts. The 18 in Krypton's "2, 8, 18, 8" becomes "12 + 6" (dodecahedron faces + octahedron vertices), and the 32 in Radon becomes "12 + 20" (dodecahedron + icosahedron faces).
