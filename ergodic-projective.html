§ 1

The irrational rotation

Fix $\alpha \notin \mathbb{Q}$ and define the sequence $x_0 = 0$, $x_{n+1} = x_n + \alpha \pmod{1}$. By Weyl's equidistribution theorem, the points $\{x_n\}$ are dense and equidistributed in $[0,1)$ for every irrational $\alpha$. Map to the circle by

$$P_n = \bigl(\cos 2\pi x_n,\; \sin 2\pi x_n\bigr) = \bigl(\cos 2\pi n\alpha,\; \sin 2\pi n\alpha\bigr).$$

The trail $\{P_n\}$ is an ergodic orbit of the circle rotation $R_\alpha : \theta \mapsto \theta + 2\pi\alpha$. The choice $\alpha = \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}$ is canonical: the golden ratio conjugate has continued fraction $[0; 1, 1, 1, \ldots]$, the slowest-converging continued fraction possible, so the Farey approximations converge most slowly and the orbit fills the circle most uniformly before any near-repetition.

The best rational approximations to $\alpha$ are the Farey fractions $F_n/F_{n+1}$ where $F_n$ is the $n$-th Fibonacci number. Near-repetitions in the orbit occur precisely when $n\alpha \approx k$ for some integer $k$, i.e. when $n/k \approx 1/\alpha$ is a good rational approximant.

§ 2

Two families of rational points

Two distinct arithmetic families provide rational points that are dense in their respective conics, both visible as projective triples $[a:b:c]$.

Pythagorean triples. The conic $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ in $\mathbb{P}^2$. Stereographic projection from $(-1, 0)$ on the unit circle: a line of slope $t = p/q$ through $(-1, 0)$ meets the circle at

$$\Bigl(\tfrac{1-t^2}{1+t^2},\;\tfrac{2t}{1+t^2}\Bigr) \;\longleftrightarrow\; [p^2-q^2 : 2pq : p^2+q^2].$$

Eisenstein triples. The norm form on $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$, where $\omega = e^{2\pi i/3}$, is $N(a + b\omega) = a^2 - ab + b^2$. The conic $a^2 - ab + b^2 = c^2$ is the Eisenstein analogue. In the $(a, b)$ plane with $c = 1$, this is the ellipse

$$a^2 - ab + b^2 = 1,$$

whose quadratic form has matrix $M = \bigl(\begin{smallmatrix}1 & -\frac{1}{2} \\ -\frac{1}{2} & 1\end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$ with eigenvalues $\frac{1}{2}$ and $\frac{3}{2}$: semi-axes $\sqrt{2}$ and $\sqrt{2/3}$, tilted at $45°$. The lattice $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$ is honest hexagonal, and the ellipse reflects the non-orthogonality of the basis $\{1, \omega\}$. Stereographic from the rational point $(1, 0)$ with slope $t = p/q$ gives

$$a(t) = \frac{t^2 - t + 1 - t(1-t)}{t^2 - t + 1}\cdot 1, \quad b(t) = \frac{2t - 1}{t^2 - t + 1},$$

yielding the Eisenstein triple $[a:b:c]$ for each rational $t$.

The ergodic trail is irrational, continuous, and fills everything. The rational points are countable, discrete, and arithmetically structured. Both are dense in the same space. That the two kinds of density coexist is one of the deeper facts the diagram shows.
