The utility claim: Rumsfeld assumes known knowns are safe. The inversion reveals they are hollow. The ansatz rebuilds them under deliberate constraint. But column IV names an irreducible condition: any criterion for authentic uncertainty becomes a performance target. A fifth rubric dimension — "demonstrates genuine epistemic discomfort" — is immediately gameable. This is Goodhart's Law applied to epistemology. The ansatz remains the best available response, but it operates under the permanent knowledge that its own success will produce a new generation of fluent performers who have learned to reconstruct ladders without ever needing one. "Just enough education to perform" is not only the diagnosis. It is the recursion.
