Special Relativity: Space, Time & E = mc2

Two postulates. One constant speed. The entire structure of space and time follows -- including the most famous equation in physics.

Postulates Simultaneity The gamma Factor Time Dilation Length Contraction Lorentz Velocity Addition Paradoxes E = mc2 Evidence

What this section is about

Special relativity begins with a fact and a principle. The fact: the Michelson-Morley experiment (1887) showed that the speed of light is the same for all observers, regardless of their motion. The principle: the laws of physics are the same in all inertial (non-accelerating) reference frames. From these two statements alone, Einstein deduced that space contracts, time dilates, simultaneity is relative, and mass is energy.

This learner covers the full IB HL treatment: the two postulates, the Lorentz factor gamma, time dilation, length contraction, the Lorentz transformation, relativistic velocity addition, the classic paradoxes, E = mc2 and the energy-momentum relation, and the experimental evidence. Every equation is derived from the two postulates -- there are no additional assumptions.

How Fast Are You Moving Right Now?

0 m/s relative to your chair. ~400 m/s relative to Earth's centre (rotation). ~30 000 m/s relative to the Sun (orbit). ~220 000 m/s relative to the galaxy centre. ~370 000 m/s relative to the cosmic microwave background.

There is no "correct" answer. Only relative velocities are physically meaningful. No experiment performed inside a sealed lab can tell you your absolute velocity -- only your velocity relative to something else. This is the starting point of special relativity.

AHL The Two Postulates of Special Relativity

Postulate 1 (Principle of Relativity): The laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames. No experiment can distinguish one inertial frame from another.

Postulate 2 (Invariance of c): The speed of light in vacuum is the same for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the source or observer: c = 3.00 x 108 m/s.

Historical context: Before Einstein, physicists assumed light travelled through a medium called the "luminiferous ether" -- a fixed reference frame against which light's speed was defined (like sound in air). The Michelson-Morley experiment tried to detect Earth's motion through this ether by comparing light speeds in different directions. Result: no difference. Light travels at c regardless of direction. There is no ether. The speed of light is not relative to any medium.

The Michelson-Morley experiment is a "null result" -- it found nothing. Yet it is one of the most important experiments in physics. Can a negative result be as scientifically significant as a positive one? The experiment didn't find what it was looking for (ether wind), but what it didn't find was more important than what it might have. This is Popper's falsifiability in action: the ether hypothesis made a testable prediction, the prediction failed, and the hypothesis was abandoned.

Q1Why is special relativity called "special"?▶
Answer

Because it deals with the special case of inertial (non-accelerating) frames only. Einstein's general relativity (1915) extends the theory to accelerating frames and gravity. "Special" doesn't mean "important" -- it means "restricted to a specific case."

Q2A spaceship moves at 0.9c and shines a laser forward. What speed does a stationary observer measure for the laser light?▶
Answer

c. Exactly c. Not 1.9c, not 0.1c. The second postulate guarantees this. The speed of light is the same for all observers -- it doesn't add to the source's velocity. This is the fact that breaks Galilean velocity addition and forces the entire structure of special relativity.

AHL Relativity of Simultaneity

A flash of light is set off at the centre of a moving spaceship. The ship's occupant sees the light hit both ends simultaneously (equal distances, same speed c).

A stationary observer outside sees the ship moving. The back wall is approaching the flash; the front wall is retreating. Since light travels at c in their frame too, the flash hits the back wall before the front wall.

Events that are simultaneous in one frame are not simultaneous in another. This is not an optical illusion or a measurement delay -- it is a fundamental feature of spacetime. There is no absolute "now" that all observers share.

Consequence: since observers disagree on the timing of events, they must also disagree on time intervals and spatial intervals. This leads directly to time dilation and length contraction.

This is NOT about signal delay. Students sometimes think the disagreement is because light takes time to reach the observer. It isn't. Even after correcting for signal travel time, the observers still disagree on whether the events were simultaneous. The disagreement is about the structure of spacetime itself, not about the speed of information.

Q1If simultaneity is relative, does causality break down? Can effects precede causes?▶
Answer

No. The ordering of causally connected events (where one could cause the other) is preserved for all observers. Only spacelike-separated events (too far apart for light to connect them) can have their ordering reversed. Since no signal can travel faster than c, cause always precedes effect for any events that can influence each other. Relativity protects causality precisely because it sets c as the speed limit.

AHL The Lorentz Factor gamma

Nearly every relativistic formula involves one quantity:

gamma = 1 / sqrt(1 - v2/c2)

gamma is a measure of how relativistic you are. At low speeds, gamma ≈ 1 and nothing unusual happens. As v approaches c, gamma diverges to infinity.

Q1Calculate gamma for v = 0.80c.▶
Answer

gamma = 1/sqrt(1 - 0.64) = 1/sqrt(0.36) = 1/0.6 = 5/3 ≈ 1.667.

Q2Why can't a massive object reach c?▶
Answer

The total energy of a moving object is E = gamma mc2. As v approaches c, gamma approaches infinity, so the energy required becomes infinite. No finite energy source can accelerate a massive object all the way to c. You can get arbitrarily close (the LHC accelerates protons to 0.999999991c), but never reach it. Only massless particles (photons, gluons) travel at exactly c -- and they always travel at c, never faster or slower.

AHL Time Dilation: Moving Clocks Run Slow
Delta t = gamma x Delta t0

Delta t0 = proper time -- the interval measured by a clock present at both events (at rest relative to them). Any other observer measures a longer interval.

At v = 0.6c (gamma = 1.25): a clock ticking 48 seconds is observed from the outside as ticking 60 seconds. The moving clock appears to run slow -- it ticks off fewer seconds per minute of the observer's time.

To the person on the moving platform, everything is normal. They don't feel time slowing down. Their heart beats normally, their clocks tick normally, their physics experiments work normally. The dilation is only apparent to the other observer. And -- crucially -- the effect is symmetric: each observer sees the other's clock running slow.

Q1A spaceship clock ticks off 10 years at v = 0.80c. How long passes on Earth?▶
Answer

gamma = 5/3. Delta t = gamma x Delta t0 = 5/3 x 10 = 16.7 years.

The astronaut ages 10 years; Earth ages 16.7 years. When they return, they're genuinely younger than their stay-at-home twin.

Q2Muons created in the upper atmosphere have a rest-frame lifetime of 2.2 microseconds. At 0.99c, how far can they travel before decaying?▶
Answer

Without relativity: d = vt = 0.99 x 3 x 108 x 2.2 x 10-6 = 653 m. They shouldn't reach the ground from 10+ km up.

With time dilation: gamma = 7.09. Dilated lifetime = 7.09 x 2.2 = 15.6 microseconds.

d = 0.99 x 3 x 108 x 15.6 x 10-6 = 4630 m ≈ 4.6 km.

In the muon's frame, it lives 2.2 microseconds but the atmosphere is length-contracted to ~1.4 km. Both descriptions give the same answer: muons reach the ground. This is experimental confirmation of relativity, observed daily.

AHL Length Contraction: Moving Objects Are Shorter
L = L0 / gamma

L0 = proper length -- measured in the rest frame of the object. Observers in relative motion measure a shorter length along the direction of travel. Perpendicular dimensions are unaffected.

At v = 0.6c (gamma = 1.25): a 1.00 m stick appears 0.80 m long to a stationary observer. At 0.99c: it appears 0.14 m. The object isn't "squished" by a force -- the geometry of spacetime itself is different for the two observers.

"Proper" = "measured at rest", not "correct". Proper time is the shortest time interval (measured by a clock present at both events). Proper length is the longest length (measured by a ruler at rest relative to the object). Other frames measure longer times and shorter lengths. The "proper" values aren't more real -- they're just the extremes.

Time dilation and length contraction are two views of the same physics. The muon reaches the ground because (Earth frame) its lifetime is dilated, OR because (muon frame) the atmosphere is contracted. Both explanations give the same prediction. They must -- they're the same spacetime, described from different frames.

Q1A 200 m spaceship passes at 0.90c. How long does it appear to a ground observer?▶
Answer

gamma = 1/sqrt(1-0.81) = 1/sqrt(0.19) = 2.294. L = 200/2.294 = 87.2 m.

The ship appears compressed to less than half its rest length.

AHL The Lorentz Transformation

The complete recipe for converting coordinates between frames in relative motion at speed v along x:

ct' = gamma(ct - vx/c)
x' = gamma(x - vt)
y' = y    z' = z

Note: x and t mix into x' and t'. This is the mathematical expression of "space and time are not separate" -- they form a unified spacetime. Moving in space unavoidably affects your time coordinate and vice versa.

When v/c approaches 0, gamma approaches 1 and the Lorentz transformation reduces to the Galilean transformation: t' = t, x' = x - vt. Relativity contains Newtonian mechanics as a limiting case.

Q1What is the spacetime interval, and why is it important?▶
Answer

Delta s2 = (c Delta t)2 - Delta x2.

This is invariant -- all observers calculate the same value, even though they disagree on Delta t and Delta x individually. It's the relativistic replacement for "distance": the true measure of separation in spacetime. If Delta s2 > 0 (timelike), events can be causally connected. If Delta s2 < 0 (spacelike), they cannot. If Delta s2 = 0 (lightlike), a light signal connects them.

AHL Relativistic Velocity Addition

Galilean addition (v1 + v2) fails because it allows speeds greater than c. The correct formula:

vrel = (v1 + v2) / (1 + v1v2/c2)

Three key properties:

  • Reduces to Galilean for small velocities (v1v2/c2 ≈ 0).
  • Can never exceed c if both v1 and v2 are at most c.
  • If either velocity is c, the result is c. Light always travels at c, regardless of the source's motion. Set v1 = c: vrel = (c + v2)/(1 + v2/c) = c.
Q1Two spaceships approach each other, each at 0.80c relative to Earth. What is their relative speed?▶
Answer

Galilean: 0.80 + 0.80 = 1.60c (impossible).

Relativistic: v = (0.80 + 0.80)/(1 + 0.80 x 0.80) = 1.60/1.64 = 0.976c.

Always below c. The denominator (1 + v1v2/c2) is always > 1 when both speeds are positive, preventing the sum from reaching c.

Q2A spaceship at 0.90c fires a probe forward at 0.50c (relative to the ship). What speed does Earth observe for the probe?▶
Answer

v = (0.90 + 0.50)/(1 + 0.90 x 0.50) = 1.40/1.45 = 0.966c.

Galilean would give 1.40c. Relativistic addition keeps it below c.

Classic Relativity Paradoxes

These aren't contradictions -- they're puzzles that test whether you've internalised the logic of relativity.

The Twin Paradox

One twin (age 30) travels at 0.6c for 30 Earth-years and returns. gamma = 1.25, so only 24 years pass for the traveller. They return at age 54; their sibling is 60.

Resolution: The situation is NOT symmetric. The travelling twin accelerates (turns around), breaking the inertial frame condition. The stay-at-home twin remains inertial throughout. The asymmetry is real and measurable -- it's been confirmed with atomic clocks on aircraft.

The Pole-Vaulter & Barn

A runner with a 12 m pole at 0.6c enters a 10 m barn. In the barn frame, the pole contracts to 9.6 m -- it fits! In the runner's frame, the barn contracts to 8 m -- it doesn't fit!

Resolution: Relativity of simultaneity. "The back end enters before the front end exits" is frame-dependent. In one frame the barn door closes while the pole is inside; in the other, the pole pokes through before the door closes. Both are correct -- they disagree on "before" and "after" for spacelike-separated events.

Q1In the twin paradox, why can't the travelling twin say "Earth moved away from me" and claim Earth should age less?▶
Answer

Because the situation is not symmetric. The travelling twin must accelerate to turn around -- they change from one inertial frame to another. During the turnaround, they're in a non-inertial frame. The stay-at-home twin never accelerates. This asymmetry is physical and absolute -- you can detect acceleration with an accelerometer. Acceleration breaks the symmetry that would otherwise make the paradox irresolvable.

AHL E = mc2 and the Energy-Momentum Relation

The total relativistic energy of a moving particle:

E = gamma mc2

At rest (gamma = 1): E0 = mc2. Rest mass IS energy. A proton sitting still has 938.3 MeV of energy locked in its mass.

The binomial approximation shows where classical KE hides: gamma ≈ 1 + ½v2/c2 for v much less than c, so:

E = gamma mc2 ≈ mc2 + ½mv2 + ...

The second term is the familiar classical kinetic energy. Relativistic energy = rest energy + kinetic energy + higher-order corrections. The kinetic energy in relativity is therefore KE = (gamma - 1)mc2.

The full energy-momentum relation:

E2 = (pc)2 + (mc2)2

For a photon (m = 0): E = pc. For a particle at rest (p = 0): E = mc2. This is the relativistic "Pythagoras" -- energy, momentum, and rest mass form a right triangle.

Mass is NOT "converted" to energy like water to steam. Mass IS energy, measured in different units (kg vs J), connected by c2. When a nucleus undergoes fission, its products weigh less -- the "missing mass" appears as kinetic energy. But the total energy (rest mass energy + kinetic energy) is conserved throughout. E = mc2 is a unit conversion, not a transformation.

"Relativistic mass" (m = gamma m0) is outdated. Modern physics uses only rest mass (invariant mass). The quantity gamma m is not a fundamental property -- it's just total energy divided by c2. When the IB says "mass increases with speed", they mean the effective inertia increases: it gets harder to accelerate, not that the object physically gets heavier.

Q1How much energy is contained in 1.0 kg of mass?▶
Answer

E = mc2 = 1.0 x (3.0 x 108)2 = 9.0 x 1016 J = 90 PJ. Roughly the annual energy consumption of a small country, or the yield of a 21 megaton nuclear weapon. Nuclear weapons convert less than 0.1% of their mass to energy -- yet this is devastating because c2 is so enormous.

Q2A proton (m0 = 938.3 MeV/c2) has total energy 3000 MeV. What is its speed?▶
Answer

gamma = E/(m0c2) = 3000/938.3 = 3.197.

v2/c2 = 1 - 1/gamma2 = 1 - 1/10.22 = 0.9022.

v = 0.950c ≈ 2.85 x 108 m/s.

Q3What is the minimum photon energy needed to create an electron-positron pair?▶
Answer

Emin = 2mec2 = 2 x 0.511 MeV = 1.022 MeV. A gamma ray with at least this energy can spontaneously create an electron and a positron (pair production), conserving energy, momentum, and charge. This is E = mc2 running backwards -- energy becoming mass.

AHL Experimental Confirmation

Special relativity is not speculative -- it is confirmed by experiment every day:

  • Time dilation -- particle lifetimes: Accelerator particles live longer at high speed (their clocks run slow in our frame). Cosmic ray muons reach the ground despite 2.2 microsecond lifetimes. Both are routine observations.
  • Length contraction -- lunar orbit: The Moon's orbit is slightly elliptical in the direction of Earth's motion around the Sun -- length-contracted in the Sun's frame.
  • E = mc2 -- nuclear energy: Nuclear reactors and bombs convert mass to energy. The Sun fuses 4.3 million tonnes of matter to energy per second. Particle accelerators create new particles from kinetic energy.
  • GPS satellites: Correct for both special relativistic time dilation (satellites move fast, clocks slow down ~7 microseconds/day) and general relativistic effects (weaker gravity, clocks speed up ~45 microseconds/day). Without these corrections, GPS would drift by ~10 km per day.
  • Relativistic mass in accelerators: At the LHC, protons at 0.9999999c require vastly more energy per increment of speed than at low v -- exactly as gamma mc2 predicts.

Relativity makes predictions that clash violently with everyday intuition. Moving clocks run slow. Moving objects are shorter. Simultaneity is relative. Mass is energy. Yet every prediction has been confirmed experimentally. When a theory contradicts common sense but matches experiment, which should we trust? The history of physics is a series of lessons in distrusting intuition and trusting mathematics and measurement. Relativity is perhaps the most dramatic case.

Q1If relativity weren't accounted for, how wrong would GPS be after one day?▶
Answer

The net time error is about 38 microseconds per day (45 microseconds general relativistic speedup minus 7 microseconds special relativistic slowdown). In 38 microseconds, light travels 38 x 10-6 x 3 x 108 = 11.4 km. So GPS positions would drift by roughly 11 km per day -- useless for navigation within a week. Relativity is not abstract physics; it's embedded in the technology in your pocket.

Q2If you were a photon, what would you experience?▶
Answer

A massless particle at speed c has gamma = infinity. Time dilation: your clock is stopped (Delta t = gamma x Delta t0 = infinity x 0 -- formally undefined, but physically: zero proper time elapses). You arrive at your destination in the same instant you leave your source, by your clock, no matter how far you travel. A photon from a distant galaxy experiences zero elapsed time across billions of years of our time. Length contraction: the universe is contracted to zero length along your direction of travel. The photon's "experience" (if such a word applies) is that departure and arrival are the same event.
